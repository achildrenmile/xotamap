# IOTA — Islands On The Air

## What is IOTA?

Islands On The Air (IOTA) is a prestigious amateur radio programme administered by the RSGB (Radio Society of Great Britain). Founded in 1964, it promotes radio contacts with stations located on islands worldwide. IOTA is one of the oldest and most respected award programmes in amateur radio.

The programme classifies islands into over 1,200 IOTA groups, each with a unique reference number (e.g. EU-003 for the Canary Islands). Groups are organised by continent and sequential number. The focus is on island groups rather than individual islands — any island within a group qualifies equally.

IOTA activations often require significant logistical effort, as many islands are difficult to reach. DXpeditions to rare IOTA groups are among the highlights of amateur radio.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** 1 (no minimum)
- **Activation zone:** On the island
- **Equipment:** Any setup, fixed or portable
- **Operating modes:** SSB, CW, FM, all digital modes
- **Special requirements:** The operator must be physically on a qualifying island. QSL card or electronic confirmation required for awards.

## Points & Awards

| Level | Requirement |
|-------|-------------|
| IOTA 100 | 100 IOTA groups worked and confirmed |
| IOTA 200 | 200 IOTA groups worked and confirmed |
| IOTA 500 | 500 IOTA groups worked and confirmed |
| IOTA 750 | 750 IOTA groups worked and confirmed |

## Getting Started

1. **Register:** Create an account at [iota-world.org](https://www.iota-world.org).
2. **Find IOTA groups:** Browse the IOTA database for island groups.
3. **Start as a chaser:** Work IOTA stations from home. Watch for DXpeditions and contests.
4. **Collect QSL cards:** Confirmation via Club Log is required for IOTA awards.
5. **Plan your own activation:** Plan a trip to an island and activate it for IOTA.

## Austria-specific Notes

Austria is a landlocked country with no IOTA references of its own. Austrian operators can participate as chasers and work IOTA stations worldwide. For activations, OE stations need to travel abroad — popular destinations include the Croatian islands (EU-016, EU-170), Greek islands, or Baltic Sea islands.
