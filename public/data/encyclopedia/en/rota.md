# ROTA — Railways On The Air

## What is ROTA?

Railways On The Air (ROTA) is an annual amateur radio event organised by the Radio Friends of the Railway Group (RFRG) in the United Kingdom. With around 150 references, the programme covers heritage railways, museum railways and stations that are activated each year in September. ROTA combines the enthusiasm for railway history with amateur radio.

The programme was created to raise awareness of the importance of preserving heritage railways. The United Kingdom is the birthplace of the railway, and numerous volunteer-run museum railways keep this heritage alive. During the ROTA weekend, radio amateurs set up their stations at historic stations, in signal boxes or even in railway carriages and transmit from these special locations. The event attracts dozens of stations each year and generates great interest in both the radio and railway communities.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** No formal minimum; participation on the event weekend counts
- **Activation Zone:** On the premises of the registered railway or station
- **Equipment:** Portable or club station equipment; coordination with the railway operator required
- **Operating Modes:** All modes permitted (SSB, CW, FM, digital modes)
- **Special Requirements:** Registration via the RFRG website before the event; strictly observe safety regulations on railway premises

## Points & Awards

| Award Level | Requirement |
|---|---|
| ROTA Participant | Participation in the annual event |
| ROTA Bronze | 15 different railway stations contacted |
| ROTA Silver | 30 different railway stations contacted |
| ROTA Gold | 50 different railway stations contacted |
| Activator Certificate | Successful activation of a railway station |

## Getting Started

1. Visit the RFRG website at rfrg.org.uk and find out about the next ROTA weekend (usually September)
2. Choose a heritage railway or station from the reference list
3. Contact the railway operator early to arrange access and a location for the radio station
4. Register your participation with the RFRG and receive your reference number
5. Activate the station on the ROTA weekend and submit your log to the RFRG
