# POTA — Parks On The Air

## What is POTA?

Parks On The Air (POTA) is an international amateur radio programme that encourages radio operators to get outdoors and operate from public parks and protected areas. Since its founding in 2017 (as a successor to the ARRL's NPOTA programme), POTA has grown explosively and is now, alongside SOTA, one of the most popular outdoor radio programmes worldwide.

The programme includes over 50,000 registered parks across more than 70 countries. Each park has a unique reference number (e.g. OE-0001 for the Hohe Tauern National Park in Austria). POTA is particularly beginner-friendly, as no mountaineering skill is required — you simply need to be within the park boundaries.

Unlike SOTA, where the ascent is part of the challenge, POTA focuses on the nature experience. Many activators combine POTA with picnics, camping, or walks. Park-to-park contacts (P2P) are especially valued.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** 10 contacts for a valid activation
- **Activation zone:** Within the official park boundaries
- **Equipment:** Portable or mobile operation from within the park
- **Operating modes:** SSB, CW, FM, FT8, FT4, all digital modes
- **Special requirements:** The operator must be physically within the park boundaries. Park-to-park (P2P) contacts count double.

## Getting Started

1. **Register:** Create a free account at [pota.app](https://pota.app).
2. **Find a park:** Use the [POTA map](https://pota.app/#/map) to locate parks near you.
3. **Equipment:** A handheld radio for VHF or a QRP transceiver for HF with a simple antenna will do.
4. **Spot yourself:** Post a spot on [pota.app](https://pota.app/#/spots) once you are QRV.
5. **Upload your log:** After the activation, upload your ADIF log at [pota.app](https://pota.app/#/activations/upload).

## Austria-specific Notes

Austria has numerous POTA references, including national parks (Hohe Tauern, Gesäuse, Neusiedler See, Donau-Auen, Kalkalpen, Thayatal), nature parks, and protected landscape areas. Many Austrian POTA parks overlap with OEFF references (WWFF), so a single activation can count for both programmes simultaneously.
