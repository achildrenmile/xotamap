# WAI — Worked All Ireland

## What is WAI?

Worked All Ireland (WAI) is an amateur radio programme that covers the entire island of Ireland — both the Republic of Ireland (prefix EI) and Northern Ireland (prefix GI). The programme is based on the Irish Grid Reference System and divides the island into grid squares that serve as references for radio activations.

With around 400 references, WAI offers a manageable but appealing collection of activatable areas. The programme is particularly noteworthy because it bridges political borders and treats the entire island as a single radio-geographic unit. Radio amateurs from both parts of the country work together on this programme, highlighting the unifying character of amateur radio.

WAI is aimed at both Irish radio amateurs who want to systematically activate their home island and international stations looking to collect Irish references. The rural nature of large parts of Ireland makes portable activations especially rewarding, as many squares can only be reached through dedicated expeditions.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** No fixed minimum for a valid activation
- **Activation Zone:** Within the respective Irish grid square
- **Equipment:** No restrictions; all power levels permitted
- **Operating Modes:** All common modes (SSB, CW, FM, digital modes)
- **Special Requirements:** The WAI reference number must be correctly provided; QSL cards or electronic confirmations serve as proof

## Points & Awards

| Award | Requirement |
|-------|-------------|
| WAI Bronze | 50 different WAI squares worked |
| WAI Silver | 100 different WAI squares worked |
| WAI Gold | 200 different WAI squares worked |
| WAI All Ireland | All available WAI references worked |
| WAI Activator Award | Specified number of WAI squares personally activated |

## Getting Started

1. Gather information on the official WAI page and study the reference list
2. Determine your own Irish grid square or select a target area for an activation
3. Make QSOs providing the WAI reference
4. Submit logs and document worked squares
5. Systematically collect further squares — particularly rare rural references are in high demand
