# JOTA — Jamboree On The Air

## What is JOTA?

Jamboree On The Air (JOTA) is the world's largest amateur radio event for young people and has been organised annually by WOSM (World Organization of the Scout Movement) since 1958. It takes place on the third full weekend in October and brings together Scout groups from over 170 countries who connect with each other via amateur radio. Millions of Scouts have participated in JOTA since its founding.

JOTA gives Scouts the opportunity to have an international experience without travel costs — they speak directly with peers around the world. Licensed radio amateurs provide the equipment and supervise the stations. Since the 1990s, JOTA has been held jointly with JOTI (Jamboree On The Internet), which adds communication via internet platforms. Together, JOTA-JOTI is the largest digital Scout event worldwide.

Unlike most xOTA programmes, JOTA is not based on reference numbers or the activation of specific locations. The focus is on the unifying spirit of the Scout movement: making contacts, learning about cultures and developing technical skills. Each participating Scout group registers on the JOTA-JOTI platform and receives its own JOTA-JOTI certificate.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** No minimum number — every contact counts
- **Activation Zone:** At a Scout group station (Scout hall, camp, school, etc.)
- **Equipment:** Any amateur radio setup; club stations or portable stations are commonly used
- **Operating Modes:** SSB, CW, FM, all digital modes — SSB on HF is the most popular
- **Special Requirements:** The station must be operated by a licensed radio amateur. Scouts without a licence may speak on the microphone under supervision (depending on national regulations). Registration via the JOTA-JOTI platform is recommended.

## For Hunters/Chasers

JOTA does not have a formal hunter/chaser programme with awards for worked contacts. However, licensed radio amateurs who contact JOTA Scout stations during the October weekend are warmly welcomed — every QSO contributes to the Scout group's experience and global participation count. Many operators make a point of working as many JOTA stations as possible during the event weekend.

No log submission or chaser certificate is issued, but contacts with JOTA stations can be noted in a personal log. JOTA station spots and frequencies are published on the JOTA-JOTI platform at jotajoti.info in advance of the event.

## Points & Awards

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Participation Certificate | For every participating Scout group |
| JOTA Host Badge | For radio amateurs who supervise a JOTA station |
| JOTA-JOTI Report | Groups submit a report after the event |

JOTA does not award traditional points or diplomas like other amateur radio programmes. The emphasis is on the experience and the educational value for Scouts.

## Getting Started

1. **Find a Scout group:** Contact a local Scout group or an amateur radio club that supports JOTA.
2. **Register:** Sign up your station at [jotajoti.info](https://www.jotajoti.info) — all organisational details are available there.
3. **Prepare your station:** Set up an amateur radio station, ideally with HF (SSB) for international contacts and VHF (FM) for local connections.
4. **Get on the air:** Be QRV on the JOTA frequencies on the third October weekend and make Scout contacts.
5. **Submit a report:** After the event, upload a JOTA-JOTI report on the official platform.
