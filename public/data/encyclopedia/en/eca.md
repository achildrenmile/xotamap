# ECA — English Castles Awards

## What is ECA?

The English Castles Awards (ECA) is a sub-programme of Global Mountain Activity (GMA) dedicated to the activation of castles and stately homes in England. With around 1,000 registered references, the programme covers an impressive variety of historic fortifications — from Norman castle ruins and medieval defensive structures to magnificent Tudor mansions.

The programme combines the fascination with English history and amateur radio, allowing operators to experience these historic sites in a unique way. Many of the listed castles are in scenic locations, adding to the appeal of an activation. ECA is managed through the GMA platform and uses its proven infrastructure for reference management and logging.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** 1 QSO for a valid activation
- **Activation Zone:** Within the immediate area of the castle or its grounds
- **Equipment:** Portable equipment recommended; maximum transmit power per licence class
- **Operating Modes:** All modes permitted (SSB, CW, FM, digital modes)
- **Special Requirements:** Observe access permissions from property owners; many castles are owned by English Heritage or the National Trust and require an entrance fee

## Points & Awards

| Award Level | Requirement |
|---|---|
| ECA Bronze | 25 different castles contacted |
| ECA Silver | 50 different castles contacted |
| ECA Gold | 100 different castles contacted |
| Activator Bronze | 10 different castles activated |
| Activator Silver | 25 different castles activated |
| Activator Gold | 50 different castles activated |

## Getting Started

1. Register for free on the GMA website at cqgma.org
2. Search the ECA reference database for castles near you or at your travel destination
3. Plan your activation — check opening hours, access conditions and parking options
4. Complete at least 1 QSO and log the activation via the GMA portal
5. Collect points as an activator or hunter and work towards the various award levels
