# ELA — English Lighthouses Awards

## What is ELA?

The English Lighthouses Awards (ELA) is a sub-programme of Global Mountain Activity (GMA) dedicated to the activation of lighthouses in England. With around 200 registered references, the programme covers historic and active lighthouses along the entire English coast — from the dramatic cliffs of Cornwall to the flat shores of East Anglia.

Lighthouses have always held a special fascination, and the ELA programme combines this maritime allure with amateur radio. Many of the listed lighthouses offer excellent propagation conditions thanks to their exposed coastal locations. Activating a lighthouse is often a remarkable experience, as the sites are frequently remote and scenically spectacular. The programme is coordinated through the GMA platform.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** 1 QSO for a valid activation
- **Activation Zone:** Within the immediate vicinity of the lighthouse (within line of sight)
- **Equipment:** Portable equipment recommended; vertical antennas are particularly well-suited for coastal locations
- **Operating Modes:** All modes permitted (SSB, CW, FM, digital modes)
- **Special Requirements:** Some lighthouses are only accessible by boat or have restricted access; pay attention to tides and weather conditions

## For Hunters/Chasers

Hunters work English lighthouse stations from home or any fixed location, counting each unique lighthouse reference toward their chaser awards. When a lighthouse activator is on the air, hunters call in and log the reference. Contacts are uploaded via the GMA portal at cqgma.org, which tracks worked references automatically.

| Award | Requirement |
|-------|-------------|
| ELA Bronze | 15 different lighthouses contacted |
| ELA Silver | 30 different lighthouses contacted |
| ELA Gold | 60 different lighthouses contacted |

Active ELA spots are visible on the GMA website and standard DX cluster networks.

## Getting Started

1. Register for free on the GMA website at cqgma.org
2. Browse the ELA reference database for lighthouses along the English coast
3. Research access options — some lighthouses are located on islands or in restricted areas
4. Activate the lighthouse with at least 1 QSO and upload your log via the GMA portal
5. Combine ELA activations with other coastal programmes for maximum on-air activity
