# WLOTA — World Lighthouse On The Air

## What is WLOTA?

World Lighthouse On The Air (WLOTA) is an international amateur radio programme promoting operating from lighthouses worldwide. The programme is based on the ARLHS World List of Lights (WLOL), a comprehensive database of over 8,000 lighthouses and lightships.

Lighthouses have always held a special fascination, and WLOTA combines this with amateur radio. The programme is active year-round and complements the related event programme ILLW, which takes place on a single weekend in August.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** 1 contact
- **Activation zone:** At or near the lighthouse
- **Equipment:** Any setup
- **Operating modes:** SSB, CW, FM, all digital modes
- **Special requirements:** The lighthouse must be registered in the ARLHS World List of Lights (WLOL).

## Points & Awards

| Level | Requirement |
|-------|-------------|
| WLOTA Bronze | 100 lighthouses worked |
| WLOTA Silver | 200 lighthouses worked |
| WLOTA Gold | 500 lighthouses worked |

## Getting Started

1. **Find a lighthouse:** Search the [WLOL database](https://wlol.arlhs.com).
2. **Get permission:** Some lighthouses require permission for access.
3. **Activate:** Operate from the lighthouse.
4. **Submit your log:** Via the WLOTA website.

## Austria-specific Notes

Austria is landlocked and has no lighthouses of its own. Austrian operators can participate as chasers or activate lighthouses abroad.
