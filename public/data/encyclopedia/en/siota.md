# SiOTA — Silos On The Air

## What is SiOTA?

Silos On The Air (SiOTA) is a unique Australian amateur radio programme that focuses on grain silos as activation locations. Australia has thousands of silos scattered across the rural hinterland, often serving as prominent landmarks in the vast plains of the continent. With around 700 registered references, SiOTA offers a substantial number of activatable sites.

Particularly appealing is the connection between SiOTA and the Australian Silo Art Trail: in recent years, numerous silos have been painted with large-scale murals by artists and have become popular tourist attractions. These painted silos draw visitors from around the world and form a dedicated tourist route through the Australian Outback. SiOTA thus combines amateur radio with art and regional tourism.

The programme encourages the exploration of rural Australia and takes radio amateurs to places they might never otherwise visit. The often remote location of the silos provides a quiet HF environment that can offer excellent radio conditions.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** 1 QSO — any contact counts
- **Activation Zone:** At or near the silo
- **Equipment:** No restrictions; portable operation is common
- **Operating Modes:** All modes permitted (SSB, CW, FT8, FM, etc.)
- **Special Requirements:** The activated silo must be registered in the SiOTA database; a photo from the location is appreciated

## For Hunters/Chasers

Hunters work SiOTA activators from home or any location, accumulating unique silo references toward chaser awards. Each silo reference worked counts once, regardless of how many times it is contacted. Hunters log their QSOs and submit them via the SiOTA website at silosontheair.com.

| Award | Requirement |
|-------|-------------|
| SiOTA Bronze | 10 different silo references worked |
| SiOTA Silver | 25 different silo references worked |
| SiOTA Gold | 50 different silo references worked |
| SiOTA Platinum | 100 different silo references worked |
| Silo Art Award | All painted silos on the Silo Art Trail worked |

Active SiOTA spots are announced on the SiOTA platform and standard DX cluster networks.

## Getting Started

1. Visit the official website silosontheair.com and explore the silo database
2. Identify silos in your region or along a planned travel route
3. Prepare a portable station (battery, antenna, transceiver)
4. Drive to the silo, set up your station and announce the activation on the SiOTA platform
5. Make QSOs, log them and optionally upload a photo of the silo to the database
