# SCOTA — Scout Camps On The Air

## What is SCOTA?

Scout Camps On The Air (SCOTA) is an amateur radio program that encourages licensed radio amateurs to make two-way contacts with Scout camps. The program is coordinated by the K2BSA Amateur Radio Association and targets registered Scouts (youth and adults) of the Boy Scouts of America (BSA).

SCOTA combines the scouting tradition of outdoor experience with amateur radio. Camps are activated year-round, with special points awarded for Jamboree stations and High Adventure camps.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** 1 QSO (any contact counts)
- **Activation zone:** Within scout camp boundaries
- **Equipment:** Any amateur radio setup at a camp station
- **Operating modes:** SSB, CW, FM, all digital modes
- **Special requirements:** Camps must be activated by registered Scouts. Approval from camp ranger or director required.

## Points & Awards

Points vary by station type:

| Station Type | Points |
|-------------|--------|
| World Jamboree Station | 10 |
| National Jamboree Station | 9 |
| SSOTA High Adventure Region | 8 |
| SCOTA High Adventure Station | 7 |
| SSOTA Challenged Council Region | 6 |
| SCOTA Council Station | 5 |
| SSOTA Council Region | 4 |

| Award | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| SCOTA Award | 50 points (25 must be from council stations) |
| Quality Camp Station | Silver/Gold for BSA camps with permanent stations |
| Quality Scout Station | Silver/Gold for individual Radio Scout stations |

## Getting Started

1. **Learn:** Visit [scota.us](https://scota.us) for rules and camp list.
2. **Find a camp:** Locate a registered BSA camp near you.
3. **Get approval:** Obtain permission from the camp ranger or director.
4. **Activate:** Operate from the camp and log your QSOs.
5. **Claim your award:** Submit your log for the SCOTA Award.

## Austria-specific Notes

SCOTA is a US-only program run by the Boy Scouts of America. For Austrian operators, it is primarily interesting as a chaser — contacts with SCOTA stations count towards the award. The related program JOTA (Jamboree On The Air) is international and therefore also relevant in Austria.
