# BiWOTA — British Inland Waterways On The Air

## What is BiWOTA?

British Inland Waterways On The Air (BiWOTA) is an annual amateur radio event in the United Kingdom that brings together radio amateurs who are boaters, cyclists, walkers, or other users of canals, rivers, towpaths, and riverbanks. The event is supported by the RSGB (Radio Society of Great Britain).

BiWOTA traditionally takes place on the August bank holiday weekend. Many clubs and individual operators use Special Event Station callsigns along British inland waterways. Mobile and portable stations using their own callsigns are equally welcome.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** 1 QSO (any contact counts)
- **Activation zone:** On or near a UK inland waterway (canal, river, towpath, riverbank)
- **Equipment:** Any portable or mobile setup
- **Operating modes:** SSB, CW, FM, all digital modes
- **Special requirements:** Open to all amateurs who use British inland waterways. Special Event Station callsigns can be applied for.

## Points & Awards

| Award | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| BiWOTA Certificate | Certificate for participation in the annual event |

## Getting Started

1. **Note the date:** BiWOTA takes place on the August bank holiday weekend (late August).
2. **Choose a waterway:** Find a canal, river, or towpath in the UK.
3. **Register:** Sign up your station on the BiWOTA website.
4. **Activate:** Operate from the waterway and log your QSOs.
5. **Report results:** Submit your log after the event.

## Austria-specific Notes

BiWOTA is a UK-only event centred on British inland waterways. For Austrian operators, it is interesting as a chaser — contacts with BiWOTA stations during the August bank holiday weekend offer a great opportunity for UK contacts. Lakes and reservoirs also count as inland waterways in the BiWOTA context.
