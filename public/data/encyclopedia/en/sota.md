# SOTA — Summits On The Air

## What is SOTA?

Summits On The Air (SOTA) is one of the world's most popular outdoor amateur radio programmes. Founded in 2002 in the United Kingdom, it encourages radio amateurs to climb mountain summits and make radio contacts from the top. SOTA combines two passions: hillwalking and amateur radio.

The programme encompasses over 100,000 classified summits across more than 100 associations worldwide. Each summit has a unique reference number and a point value based on its topographic prominence. Points range from 1 (for summits with 150m+ prominence) to 10 (for summits with 1000m+ prominence).

SOTA distinguishes between activators (who operate from the summit) and chasers (who contact the activator from home or elsewhere). Both sides earn points for their contacts.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** 4 contacts for a valid activation
- **Activation zone:** Within 25 vertical metres of the summit
- **Equipment:** All equipment must be carried on foot — no motorised access
- **Operating modes:** SSB, CW, FM, FT8, all digital modes
- **Special requirements:** The activator must ascend the summit under their own power. No vehicles, cable cars, or other motorised transport to the activation zone.

## Points & Awards

| Level | Points |
|-------|--------|
| Summit 1 point | 150m+ prominence |
| Summit 2 points | 300m+ prominence |
| Summit 4 points | 500m+ prominence |
| Summit 6 points | 650m+ prominence |
| Summit 8 points | 800m+ prominence |
| Summit 10 points | 1000m+ prominence |
| **Mountain Goat** (Activator) | 1,000 activator points |
| **Shack Sloth** (Chaser) | 1,000 chaser points |
| **Super Sloth** | 10,000 points |

Winter bonus: Between 1 December and 15 March, an additional 3 bonus points per activation are awarded (Northern Hemisphere).

## Getting Started

1. **Register:** Create a free account at [sotadata.org.uk](https://sotadata.org.uk).
2. **Find summits:** Use [SOTAmaps](https://sotamaps.org) or the SOTA app to find summits near you.
3. **Equipment:** A simple handheld radio (e.g. on 2m/70cm) is enough to get started. For HF, a QRP transceiver (e.g. Elecraft KX2, Yaesu FT-818, Xiegu G90) with a lightweight wire antenna works well.
4. **Spot yourself:** Before or during the activation, post a spot on [SOTAwatch](https://sotawatch.sota.org.uk) to alert chasers.
5. **Upload your log:** After the activation, upload your log to [SOTAdata](https://sotadata.org.uk).

## Austria-specific Notes

Austria is divided into 9 SOTA associations, corresponding to its federal states (e.g. OE/TI for Tyrol, OE/SB for Salzburg). Thanks to its alpine topography, Austria offers outstanding SOTA opportunities — from easy hiking summits to challenging high-alpine peaks. Many Austrian summits carry high point values (8 or 10 points).
