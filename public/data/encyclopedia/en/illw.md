# ILLW — International Lighthouse Lightship Weekend

## What is ILLW?

The International Lighthouse Lightship Weekend (ILLW) is an annual amateur radio event held on the third full weekend of August. It is one of the largest amateur radio events worldwide, with over 500 participating lighthouses across 95 countries per event.

ILLW was founded in 1998, evolving from the Scottish Northern Lighthouse Weekend. The event promotes operating from lighthouses and lightships while raising awareness of their historical significance and preservation.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** 1 contact
- **Activation zone:** At or near the lighthouse/lightship
- **Equipment:** Any setup
- **Operating modes:** SSB, CW, FM, all digital modes
- **Special requirements:** Event takes place on the third full weekend of August. Registration on the ILLW website required.

## Getting Started

1. **Mark the date:** Third full weekend of August.
2. **Find a lighthouse:** Search [illw.net](https://illw.net) for a lighthouse near you.
3. **Register:** Register your participation on the ILLW website.
4. **Get permission:** Arrange access to the lighthouse in advance.
5. **Activate:** Operate from the lighthouse on the event weekend.

## Austria-specific Notes

Austria has no lighthouses of its own, but Austrian operators regularly participate as chasers. For an activation, lighthouses in neighbouring countries are a good option.
