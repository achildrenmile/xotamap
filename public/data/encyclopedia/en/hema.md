# HEMA — HuMPs Excluding Marilyns Award

## What is HEMA?

HuMPs Excluding Marilyns Award (HEMA) is an amateur radio programme for summits with topographic prominence between 100 and 149 metres. The name refers to HuMPs (Hundred Metre Prominences) — hills that narrowly miss the Marilyn criteria (150m+ prominence, as used in SOTA).

HEMA was founded in the UK and has expanded to 19 additional DXCC entities. The programme bridges the gap between GMA (all mountains) and SOTA (only summits with 150m+ prominence), offering additional targets for hill-loving operators.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** 1 contact (no minimum)
- **Activation zone:** Within 25 vertical metres of the summit
- **Equipment:** Portable, summit reached on foot
- **Operating modes:** SSB, CW, FM, all digital modes
- **Special requirements:** Summit must have between 100m and 149m topographic prominence. Similar rules to SOTA.

## Getting Started

1. **Find summits:** Search [hema.org.uk](http://hema.org.uk) for summits with GPS waypoints.
2. **Read the rules:** Similar to SOTA — summit must be reached on foot.
3. **Activate:** Operate from the summit area.
4. **Submit your log:** Via the HEMA website.

## Austria-specific Notes

Austria has numerous hills meeting HEMA criteria, especially in the Alpine foothills and lower mountain ranges. These summits complement the SOTA offering with smaller, often more accessible targets.
