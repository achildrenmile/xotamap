# ZLOTA — ZL On The Air

## What is ZLOTA?

ZL On The Air (ZLOTA) is a New Zealand amateur radio programme that brings together various reference types under a single umbrella. The programme encompasses summits, national parks, lakes, lighthouses and other notable locations in New Zealand (ZL), offering radio amateurs a versatile platform for portable activations.

ZLOTA was created to consolidate and unify the many existing activation programmes in New Zealand. Instead of maintaining a separate system for each reference type, radio amateurs can use ZLOTA to log and manage all their activities centrally. The database comprises around 1,500 references spread across both main islands and the surrounding areas of New Zealand.

The programme is popular with both New Zealand radio amateurs and international chasers. New Zealand's spectacular scenery — from the Southern Alps to coastal lighthouses — makes ZLOTA activations a truly special experience.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** 1 (any contact counts)
- **Activation Zone:** At or near the reference
- **Equipment:** Any portable or mobile setup
- **Operating Modes:** SSB, CW, FM, all digital modes
- **Special:** Various reference types in New Zealand (summits, parks, lighthouses, etc.)

## Points & Awards

| Award | Requirement |
|-------|-------------|
| ZLOTA Bronze | 50 references contacted |
| ZLOTA Silver | 150 references contacted |
| ZLOTA Gold | 300 references contacted |
| Activator Awards | Tiered by number of references activated |

## Getting Started

1. Visit the ZLOTA website at ontheair.nz and create an account
2. Study the available reference types and their rules
3. Choose a nearby reference and plan your activation
4. Set up your portable station, make the required QSOs and enjoy the radio operations
5. Log your activation on the ZLOTA platform
