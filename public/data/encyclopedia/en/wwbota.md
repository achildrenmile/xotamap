# WWBOTA — Worldwide Bunkers On The Air

## What is WWBOTA?

Worldwide Bunkers On The Air (WWBOTA) is an international amateur radio programme that promotes operating from bunkers, military installations, and fortifications. Founded in Europe, the programme has rapidly expanded to over 23 countries with more than 15,000 registered bunkers.

Each country has its own national sub-programme: OEBOTA for Austria, DLBOTA for Germany, UKBOTA for the UK, and so on. The bunkers date from various eras — from medieval fortresses through World War I defences to Cold War bunkers.

WWBOTA features a modern API with Server-Sent Events for real-time spots and a comprehensive reference database with coordinates.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** 5 contacts for a valid activation
- **Activation zone:** At or in direct proximity to the bunker
- **Equipment:** Portable operation
- **Operating modes:** SSB, CW, FM, FT8, all digital modes
- **Special requirements:** Photo documentation is encouraged. Observe safety precautions — many bunkers are in poor condition.

## Getting Started

1. **Find bunkers:** Browse the [WWBOTA database](https://wwbota.net/bunkers) for bunkers near you.
2. **Safety first:** Check the condition of the bunker beforehand. Do not enter unsafe structures.
3. **Equipment:** A portable setup with an antenna suitable for the bunker location.
4. **Activate:** Make at least 5 QSOs from the bunker.
5. **Submit your log:** Upload your log at [wwbota.net](https://wwbota.net).

## Austria-specific Notes

Austria has numerous registered bunkers under the OEBOTA designation, including imperial-era fortifications, World War II installations, and Cold War infrastructure. Carinthia, Tyrol, and Salzburg have a particularly high number of historical fortifications.
