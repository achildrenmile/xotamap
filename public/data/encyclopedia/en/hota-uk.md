# HOTA-UK — Houses On The Air

## What is HOTA-UK?

Houses On The Air (HOTA) is a British amateur radio programme that takes a particularly everyday approach: radio amateurs activate their own home or visit other registered houses to operate from there. The programme was created to offer an activation opportunity to those who cannot travel to remote locations due to health or other reasons.

With around 5,000 registered references across the United Kingdom, HOTA offers an extensive database of activatable locations. Each registered house receives a unique HOTA reference number. The concept is deliberately low-threshold — you can simply participate from your own home address without needing special equipment or a long journey.

HOTA promotes the community spirit of amateur radio by turning everyday residences into radio operating locations. It is an ideal entry point into the world of xOTA programmes, particularly for newcomers and less mobile radio amateurs.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** No minimum number for a valid activation
- **Activation Zone:** At the registered house or its immediate surroundings (garden, driveway)
- **Equipment:** No restrictions; any transmit power and antenna type permitted
- **Operating Modes:** All modes (SSB, CW, FT8, FM, etc.)
- **Special Requirements:** The house must be registered in the HOTA system and have a valid reference number

## Getting Started

1. Visit the official website hota.org.uk and create an account
2. Register your own home as a HOTA reference and receive a reference number
3. Browse the database for already registered houses in your area
4. Carry out your first activation from home and log the QSO in the HOTA system
5. Chase other HOTA stations or visit and activate additional houses
