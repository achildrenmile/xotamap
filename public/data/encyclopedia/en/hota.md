# HOTA — Heritage On The Air

## What is HOTA?

Heritage On The Air (HOTA) is a South African amateur radio programme dedicated to the activation of cultural heritage sites. With around 300 registered references, the programme covers nationally protected monuments, historic buildings, museums and significant cultural sites across South Africa. HOTA was established to raise awareness of the country's rich and diverse heritage through amateur radio.

South Africa possesses an extraordinary cultural diversity reflected in its monuments — from San rock paintings and Cape Dutch architecture to sites of apartheid history. HOTA gives radio amateurs the opportunity to visit these places while operating on the air. The programme thus promotes not only amateur radio, but also awareness of the protection and preservation of South African cultural sites. It is one of the few African xOTA programmes and has a growing international community of chasers.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** 10 QSOs for a valid activation
- **Activation Zone:** Within the grounds or in the immediate vicinity of the heritage site
- **Equipment:** Portable equipment recommended; maximum 100 watts transmit power
- **Operating Modes:** All modes permitted (SSB, CW, FM, digital modes)
- **Special Requirements:** Valid South African or CEPT-compatible amateur radio licence; obtain access permission for protected sites

## Points & Awards

| Award Level | Requirement |
|---|---|
| HOTA Bronze | 25 different sites contacted |
| HOTA Silver | 50 different sites contacted |
| HOTA Gold | 100 different sites contacted |
| Activator Bronze | 10 different sites activated |
| Activator Silver | 25 different sites activated |
| Activator Gold | 50 different sites activated |

## Getting Started

1. Visit the official HOTA website at heritageontheair.co.za and register
2. Browse the reference database for heritage sites near you or at your travel destination
3. Check access conditions and any permits required for the chosen site
4. Complete at least 10 QSOs and upload your log via the HOTA website
5. Track your progress in the online portal and work towards the award levels
