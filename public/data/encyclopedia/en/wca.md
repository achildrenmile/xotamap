# WCA — World Castles Award

## What is WCA?

The World Castles Award (WCA) is an international amateur radio programme that promotes operating from castles, palaces, fortresses, and ruins worldwide. The programme was created by the WCA group and encompasses over 55,000 registered castles across numerous countries.

Each castle has a unique WCA reference number consisting of a country prefix and sequential number (e.g. OE-00001). The related programme COTA (Castles On The Air) is often used interchangeably, with WCA being the overarching international programme.

WCA connects amateur radio with cultural heritage and history. Many activators take the opportunity to visit historic sites while making radio contacts.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** 1 contact (no minimum)
- **Activation zone:** On the castle grounds or within sight of the castle
- **Equipment:** Any portable or mobile setup
- **Operating modes:** SSB, CW, FM, all digital modes
- **Special requirements:** Photo documentation is recommended.

## Points & Awards

| Level | Requirement |

## Getting Started

1. **Find references:** Browse the [WCA reference list](https://wcagroup.org/?page_id=207) for castles near you.
2. **Equipment:** A portable setup will do — many castles are accessible by car.
3. **Activate:** Operate from the castle grounds and note the WCA reference in your log.
4. **Submit your log:** Logs are submitted through the WCA group.
5. **Apply for awards:** Apply for awards when you reach the point thresholds.

## Austria-specific Notes

Austria has a rich castle landscape with hundreds of WCA references. From medieval castle ruins to magnificent palaces, OE offers numerous opportunities for WCA activations. Lower Austria, Styria, and Carinthia have a particularly high density of castles. Additionally, COTA-OE (P14) is a dedicated Austrian castle programme under the AFCH/OVSV.
