# MOTA — Mills On The Air

## What is MOTA?

Mills On The Air (MOTA) is a sub-programme of Global Mountain Activity (GMA) dedicated to the activation of mills and windmills. With around 500 registered references, the programme covers a diverse collection of historic mill sites — from classic windmills and watermills to early industrial grinding works, predominantly in the United Kingdom and the Netherlands.

The programme celebrates the industrial and agricultural heritage of Europe. Mills were the backbone of food production for centuries and continue to shape the landscape of many regions to this day. MOTA offers radio amateurs the chance to visit these often lovingly restored structures and operate from there. Many mill associations welcome radio activities, as they bring additional attention to the preservation of these historic monuments.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** 1 QSO for a valid activation
- **Activation Zone:** Within the immediate area of the mill or on the mill grounds
- **Equipment:** Portable equipment; coordinate antenna setup with the mill operator
- **Operating Modes:** All modes permitted (SSB, CW, FM, digital modes)
- **Special Requirements:** Obtain permission from the mill owner or association; take particular care around working mills with moving parts

## Getting Started

1. Register for free on the GMA website at cqgma.org
2. Search the MOTA reference database for mills in your region
3. Contact the mill operator or association and clarify access conditions
4. Complete at least 1 QSO and upload your log via the GMA portal
5. Visit mill days and open-mill events for particularly interesting activations
