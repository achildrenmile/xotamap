# KRMNPA — Keith Roget Memorial National Parks Award

## What is KRMNPA?

The Keith Roget Memorial National Parks Award (KRMNPA) is an Australian amateur radio programme that promotes contacts from and with national parks and state parks in Australia (VK). The programme is named after Keith Roget VK3MN (SK), a dedicated radio amateur who was passionate about portable operations from Australian national parks.

KRMNPA is one of the oldest park-related amateur radio programmes in the world, established long before the internationally known POTA programme (Parks on the Air). It encompasses around 800 references covering all Australian states and territories — from the tropical rainforests of Queensland and the desert parks of the Outback to the alpine areas of the Snowy Mountains.

The programme is managed through the Parks'n'Peaks platform, which also supports other Australian outdoor activation programmes such as WWFF-VK. KRMNPA has an active and helpful community and is a well-established part of Australian amateur radio culture.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** At least 1 QSO from the park for a valid activation (higher requirements apply for awards)
- **Activation Zone:** Within the official park boundaries
- **Equipment:** No restrictions; portable equipment is common
- **Operating Modes:** All modes permitted (SSB, CW, Digital, FM)
- **Special Requirements:** Observe park management permits; some parks have restrictions on antennas or generators

## For Hunters/Chasers

Hunters work KRMNPA activators from home or any location, collecting unique Australian national and state park references toward chaser awards. Each park reference worked counts once regardless of how many times it is contacted. Chaser logs are submitted via the Parks'n'Peaks platform at parksnpeaks.org, which tracks progress automatically.

| Award | Requirement |
|-------|-------------|
| KRMNPA Bronze | 25 different parks contacted |
| KRMNPA Silver | 50 different parks contacted |
| KRMNPA Gold | 100 different parks contacted |
| Platinum | 200 different parks contacted |

Active KRMNPA spots are posted on the Parks'n'Peaks website and standard DX cluster networks.

## Getting Started

1. Visit the Parks'n'Peaks website and read the KRMNPA rules
2. Create a user account on parksnpeaks.org
3. Choose a nearby national park or state park as your target
4. Set up a portable station in the park and make contacts
5. Log your QSOs on the Parks'n'Peaks platform and submit for awards
