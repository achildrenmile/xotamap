# TOTA — Towers On The Air

## What is TOTA?

Towers On The Air (TOTA), also known as Worldwide Towers On The Air (WWTOTA), is an amateur radio programme promoting operating from observation towers and other towers. The programme originated in the Czech Republic and has expanded to several European countries, including Germany, Austria, Slovakia, and Poland.

Each country has its own national sub-programme: OER for Austria, DLR for Germany, OKR for the Czech Republic, OMR for Slovakia, SPR for Poland. The programme includes over 3,600 registered towers.

TOTA maintains an active reference database with coordinates and a map on wwtota.com.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** 3 contacts on VHF, 13 contacts on HF
- **Activation zone:** From the tower itself or within a maximum distance of 200 metres
- **Equipment:** Portable operation, self-powered
- **Operating modes:** All bands and modes permitted (SSB, CW, FM, digital modes)
- **Special requirements:** Tower must be registered in the TOTA reference database. Contacts via terrestrial repeaters are not allowed; satellite contacts are permitted. Each QSO must include callsign, report, name and QTH.

| Category | Points |
|----------|--------|

| QSO as hunter | 1 point per unique QSO |

Bronze (25), Silver (50), Gold (75) unique R2R contacts yearly.

## Getting Started

1. **Find a tower:** Search the [TOTA database](https://wwtota.com/seznam/) for towers near you.
2. **Check access:** Some towers have limited opening hours or are seasonal.
3. **Activate:** Operate from the tower or within 200m — at least 3 QSOs on VHF or 13 on HF.
4. **Submit your log:** Upload your ADIF log at [wwtota.com](https://wwtota.com) with the tower reference in the MY_SIG_INFO field.

## Austria-specific Notes

Under the OER prefix (Austrian references), numerous observation towers in Austria are registered. From fire watchtowers in the Alps to historic observation towers in the Vienna Woods, Austria offers diverse TOTA activation opportunities.
