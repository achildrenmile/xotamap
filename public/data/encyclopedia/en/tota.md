# TOTA — Towers On The Air

## What is TOTA?

Towers On The Air (TOTA), also known as Worldwide Towers On The Air (WWTOTA), is an amateur radio programme promoting operating from observation towers and other towers. The programme originated in the Czech Republic and has expanded to several European countries, including Germany, Austria, Slovakia, and Poland.

Each country has its own national sub-programme: OER for Austria, DLR for Germany, OKR for the Czech Republic, OMR for Slovakia, SPR for Poland. The programme includes over 2,000 registered towers.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** 1 contact
- **Activation zone:** At or on the tower
- **Equipment:** Portable operation
- **Operating modes:** SSB, CW, FM, all digital modes
- **Special requirements:** Tower must be in the TOTA reference database.

## Points & Awards

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| TOTA Activator Awards | Based on number of towers activated |
| TOTA Hunter Awards | Based on number of towers worked |

## Getting Started

1. **Find a tower:** Search the [TOTA database](https://wwtota.com) for towers near you.
2. **Check access:** Some towers have limited opening hours or are seasonal.
3. **Activate:** Operate from the tower or in its immediate vicinity.
4. **Submit your log:** Upload your log at [wwtota.com](https://wwtota.com).

## Austria-specific Notes

Under the OER prefix (Austrian references), numerous observation towers in Austria are registered. From fire watchtowers in the Alps to historic observation towers in the Vienna Woods, Austria offers diverse TOTA activation opportunities.
