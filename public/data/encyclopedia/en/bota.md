# BOTA — Beaches On The Air

## What is BOTA?

Beaches On The Air (BOTA) is an international amateur radio programme promoting operating from beaches worldwide. With over 30,000 registered beaches, BOTA is one of the most extensive outdoor radio programmes. The programme combines the joy of the beach with amateur radio, inviting operators to make contacts from coastal locations.

Each beach has a unique BOTA reference number. The programme is particularly popular in Australia, North America, and Europe.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** 1 contact (no minimum)
- **Activation zone:** On or directly adjacent to the beach
- **Equipment:** Any portable or mobile setup
- **Operating modes:** SSB, CW, FM, all digital modes
- **Special requirements:** Photo documentation encouraged. Salt water and sand require extra equipment protection.

## Points & Awards

| Level | Requirement |
|-------|-------------|
| BOTA Bronze | 25 beaches worked or activated |
| BOTA Silver | 50 beaches worked or activated |
| BOTA Gold | 100 beaches worked or activated |

## Getting Started

1. **Find a beach:** Visit [beachesontheair.com](https://beachesontheair.com) and search for beaches near you.
2. **Protect your equipment:** Salty air and sand can damage gear. Use protective covers.
3. **Activate:** Operate from the beach and log the BOTA reference.
4. **Submit your log:** Via the BOTA website.

## Austria-specific Notes

Austria is landlocked and has no BOTA beaches of its own. Austrian operators can participate as chasers or activate beaches abroad — popular destinations include the Croatian and Italian Adriatic coast.
