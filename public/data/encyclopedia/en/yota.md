# YOTA — Youngsters On The Air

## What is YOTA?

Youngsters On The Air (YOTA) is an international initiative of IARU Region 1 aimed at inspiring young people under 26 for amateur radio. The programme was launched in 2011 and has since become one of the most important youth initiatives in amateur radio. YOTA seeks to counter the demographic shift in amateur radio and nurture a new generation of operators.

The highlight of the YOTA year is the annual YOTA Month in December. During this month, youth groups from numerous countries are active on the bands with special YOTA callsigns — for example OE0YOTA from Austria. These special callsigns generate great attention and motivate young operators to participate in amateur radio for the first time.

In addition, YOTA organises an annual international summer camp where young radio amateurs from across Europe come together. The camp takes place in a different country each year and offers workshops, fox hunts, homebrew projects and shared radio operations. Many participants report that this camp was instrumental in fostering their passion for amateur radio.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** No minimum number for YOTA Month participants
- **Activation Zone:** No location-based restrictions — operation from anywhere is possible
- **Equipment:** Any amateur radio setup; club stations or portable stations are commonly used
- **Operating Modes:** SSB, CW, FM, FT8, FT4 and all digital modes
- **Special Requirements:** Participants should be under 26 years old. National amateur radio associations apply for special YOTA callsigns for the YOTA Month. Experienced radio amateurs may assist as mentors.

## Points & Awards

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| YOTA Month Participation | Certificate for active YOTA stations in December |
| YOTA Month Award | For chasers who work multiple YOTA special stations |
| YOTA Summer Camp Diploma | For participants of the annual summer camp |

During the YOTA Month, both YOTA stations and chasers can apply for awards. The exact conditions are announced on the YOTA website each year.

## Getting Started

1. **Learn more:** Visit [ham-yota.com](https://ham-yota.com) for all current YOTA activities and the next YOTA Month.
2. **Contact your national association:** Get in touch with your national amateur radio society to find out if a YOTA station is planned in your country.
3. **Get on the air in December:** During YOTA Month, search the bands for YOTA special stations or become active yourself as a young operator.
4. **Apply for summer camp:** Apply through your national association for the annual YOTA Summer Camp — places are limited and highly sought after.
5. **Build your network:** Stay in touch with other young radio amateurs through social media and the YOTA community.
