# WWFF — World Wide Flora & Fauna

## What is WWFF?

World Wide Flora & Fauna (WWFF) is an international amateur radio programme that promotes operating from protected nature areas and raises awareness about conservation of flora and fauna. Officially founded in 2009, WWFF has grown into one of the largest programmes with over 40,700 references across 172 countries.

Each participating country has its own national programme with a dedicated prefix — OEFF for Austria, DLFF for Germany, VKFF for Australia, and so on. References are based on officially recognised protected areas such as national parks, nature parks, Natura 2000 sites, and other conservation areas.

WWFF differs from POTA through its higher minimum QSO requirement (44 instead of 10) and its close ties to officially protected natural areas. Many areas hold both POTA and WWFF references.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** 44 contacts for a valid activation
- **Activation zone:** Within the official boundaries of the protected area
- **Equipment:** Portable operation from within the protected area
- **Operating modes:** SSB, CW, FM, all digital modes
- **Special requirements:** 44 QSOs are required for a valid activation. The operator must be within the boundaries of an officially recognised protected area.

## Getting Started

1. **Learn:** Visit the [WWFF website](https://wwff.co) and read the rules.
2. **Find a reference:** Search the [WWFF directory](https://wwff.co/directory/) for protected areas near you.
3. **Equipment:** A QRP transceiver with a wire antenna for HF, or a handheld for VHF.
4. **Activate:** Make at least 44 QSOs. Spot yourself on [GMA](https://www.cqgma.org) or via Spothole.
5. **Submit your log:** Submit your ADIF log to the national WWFF coordinator.

## Austria-specific Notes

The OEFF (Austrian Flora-Fauna Award) is Austria's national WWFF sub-programme. References carry the prefix OEFF-nnnn. Austria offers numerous protected areas across its 6 national parks, nature parks, and Natura 2000 sites. Many OEFF references overlap with POTA parks — a single activation can count for both, though with different minimum QSO requirements (POTA: 10, WWFF: 44).
