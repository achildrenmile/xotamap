# GMA — Global Mountain Activity

## What is GMA?

Global Mountain Activity (GMA) is an international amateur radio programme focused on operating from mountains and hills of all sizes. Unlike SOTA, which has strict criteria regarding prominence and foot-only access, GMA is much more inclusive: smaller hills and elevations are also registered as references, and there are no restrictions on the mode of transport.

GMA was created to provide a comprehensive database of all mountains worldwide. The programme also aggregates spots from other programmes such as SOTA, WWFF, POTA, and COTA, making it an important information hub for outdoor operators. The GMA map shows references from multiple programmes on a single platform.

With over 150,000 references worldwide, GMA is the most extensive mountain database in amateur radio. There is no minimum QSO count for a valid activation — even a single contact from the summit counts.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** 1 contact (no minimum)
- **Activation zone:** Summit area
- **Equipment:** Any portable setup
- **Operating modes:** SSB, CW, FM, FT8, all digital modes
- **Special requirements:** No restriction on mode of transport. Points depend on the prominence and height of the summit.

## Getting Started

1. **Visit the website:** Go to [cqgma.org](https://www.cqgma.org) and explore the GMA map.
2. **Find a mountain:** The [GMA map](https://www.cqgma.org/gmamap/) shows all references worldwide.
3. **Equipment:** Any portable setup — from a handheld to a full HF station.
4. **Activate:** Operate from the summit and post a spot on GMA.
5. **Submit your log:** Upload your log as CSV on the GMA website.

## Austria-specific Notes

Austria's topography provides an enormous number of GMA references. Many overlap with SOTA summits, but GMA additionally includes many smaller elevations that do not meet SOTA criteria. GMA is therefore a great complement for operators who want to be active beyond SOTA summits.
