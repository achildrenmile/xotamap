# GMA — Global Mountain Activity

## What is GMA?

Global Mountain Activity (GMA) is an international platform for outdoor amateur radio that originally focused on mountains but now also encompasses castles, lighthouses, mills, and nature parks. The motto: "Outdoor radio without compromise!" — any site with a reference number can be activated in GMA.

Unlike SOTA, which has strict criteria regarding prominence and foot-only access, GMA is much more inclusive: smaller hills and elevations are registered as references, and there are no restrictions on the mode of transport. GMA also recognises references from other programmes (SOTA, WWFF, POTA, COTA, Humps Award, Lighthouses).

With over 150,000 references worldwide, GMA is the most extensive outdoor database in amateur radio. The GMA map aggregates references from many programmes on a single platform, making it an essential tool for any outdoor operator.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** No minimum — even 1 QSO from the site counts
- **Activation zone:** At or near the reference point (summit, castle, lighthouse, etc.)
- **Equipment:** Any portable or mobile setup, no restrictions
- **Operating modes:** All bands and modes permitted (SSB, CW, FM, FT8, all digital modes)
- **Special requirements:** No transport restrictions — cable car, car, bicycle all allowed. The location must have a valid GMA reference number.

## Points & Awards

For mountain activations: **1 point per 100 metres of elevation**.

| Award | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| GMA Trailblazer | Cumulative points based on elevation |
| GMA Mill Quest (MOTA) | Activating mills and windmills |
| GMA Tower Quest | Activating towers |
| GMA Top 16 DL | The 16 highest summits in Germany |
| Country-specific Awards | Awards per DXCC entity (e.g. Belgian Castles, Belgian Lighthouses) |
| Go Green XOTA | Eco-friendly outdoor activations |

## Getting Started

1. **Register:** Create a free account at [cqgma.org](https://www.cqgma.org).
2. **Find a reference:** The [GMA map](https://www.cqgma.org/gmamap/) shows all references worldwide — mountains, castles, lighthouses and more.
3. **Equipment:** Any portable setup — from a handheld to a full HF station. No restrictions.
4. **Activate:** Operate from the site and log your QSOs.
5. **Upload your log:** Upload QSOs on the GMA website and collect points.

## Austria-specific Notes

Austria's topography provides an enormous number of GMA references. Many overlap with SOTA summits, but GMA additionally includes many smaller elevations that do not meet SOTA criteria. Castles (COTA-OE) and nature parks (WWFF) are also accessible via GMA. GMA is an ideal complement for operators who want to be active beyond SOTA summits.
