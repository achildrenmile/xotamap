# LLOTA — Lagos y Lagunas On The Air

## What is LLOTA?

Lagos y Lagunas On The Air (LLOTA) is an amateur radio programme promoting operating from lakes, lagoons, and reservoirs. Originating in South America, the programme is expanding internationally. Activators must be within 200 metres of the water body's shore.

LLOTA fills a unique niche in outdoor amateur radio: operating from the shores of inland water bodies. The programme includes a reference database with GPS coordinates.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** 1 contact
- **Activation zone:** Within 200m of the lake/lagoon shore
- **Equipment:** Portable operation
- **Operating modes:** SSB, CW, FM, all digital modes
- **Special requirements:** Operator must be within 200 metres of the shore.

## Points & Awards

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| LLOTA Activator | Based on number of lakes activated |
| LLOTA Hunter | Based on number of lakes worked |

## Getting Started

1. **Find a lake:** Browse the [LLOTA reference database](https://www.outnaboot.ca/llota/).
2. **Activate:** Operate within 200m of the lakeshore.
3. **Submit your log:** Via the LLOTA website.

## Austria-specific Notes

Austria has numerous lakes suitable for LLOTA activations — from Lake Constance to Wörthersee to Neusiedler See. New reference registration is handled through the LLOTA administration.
