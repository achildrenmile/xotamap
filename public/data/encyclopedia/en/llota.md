# LLOTA — Lagos y Lagunas On The Air

## What is LLOTA?

Lagos y Lagunas On The Air (LLOTA) is an amateur radio programme that promotes operating from lakes, lagoons and reservoirs worldwide. The programme originated in South America and was further developed internationally by Canadian radio amateurs. Today, the reference database includes water bodies on all continents.

LLOTA fills a unique niche in outdoor amateur radio: operating from the shores of inland water bodies. Whether an alpine mountain lake, a tropical lagoon or a large reservoir — LLOTA motivates radio amateurs to discover the diversity of water landscapes and operate from there.

Unlike many other programmes, the activation zone is relatively generously defined: you simply need to be within 200 metres of the shore. This makes LLOTA particularly beginner-friendly and easy to combine with fishing, paddling or simply an afternoon by the lake.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** 1 contact
- **Activation Zone:** Within 200 metres of the shore of the lake, lagoon or reservoir
- **Equipment:** Portable or mobile operation
- **Operating Modes:** SSB, CW, FM, all digital modes
- **Special Requirements:** The water body must be registered in the LLOTA reference database. New water bodies can be proposed.

## Getting Started

1. **Find a lake:** Browse the [LLOTA reference database](https://www.outnaboot.ca/llota/) for registered water bodies.
2. **Choose your spot:** Find a suitable location within 200m of the lakeshore.
3. **Activate:** Set up your station and include the LLOTA reference number in your CQ call.
4. **Submit your log:** Submit your ADIF log via the LLOTA website.
5. **Propose new lakes:** If your favourite lake is not yet registered, you can propose it through the website.

## Austria-specific Notes

Austria is a paradise for LLOTA activations. With hundreds of lakes, there are countless opportunities — from Lake Constance in the west, through the Carinthian lakes (Woerthersee, Millstaetter See, Ossiacher See), to Neusiedler See in the east. Alpine reservoirs (e.g. Speicher Koelnbrein, Schlegeis Reservoir) are also appealing locations.
