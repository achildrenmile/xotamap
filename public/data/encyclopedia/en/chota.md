# CHOTA — Churches & Chapels On The Air

## What is CHOTA?

Churches & Chapels On The Air (CHOTA) is an annual amateur radio event organised by WACRAL (World Association of Christian Radio Amateurs and Listeners). It takes place on the second Saturday of September each year. During this event, radio amateurs set up portable stations in or near churches and chapels and make contacts from there.

WACRAL was founded in 1956 and combines Christian values with amateur radio. CHOTA is the flagship event of the organisation and attracts participants from numerous countries every year — primarily from the United Kingdom, but also from continental Europe, North America and other regions. The event provides an excellent opportunity to combine historic church buildings with amateur radio and to spark public interest in the hobby.

Unlike many other xOTA programmes, CHOTA does not have a formalised reference system with numbered locations. Any church or chapel can be activated — from a small village chapel to a large cathedral. Participation is open to all radio amateurs regardless of denomination or religious affiliation. Locations with historical significance or scenic settings are particularly popular.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** No formal minimum — every contact counts
- **Activation Zone:** In or in the immediate vicinity of a church or chapel (churchyard, church garden, etc.)
- **Equipment:** Portable or mobile setup, often battery-powered with wire antennas
- **Operating Modes:** SSB, CW, FM, all digital modes
- **Special Requirements:** Obtain permission from the vicar or church administration. Treat the sacred site with respect. The station must not interfere with services or church activities.

## Points & Awards

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| CHOTA Participation Certificate | For every active participant |
| CHOTA Activator | For stations that successfully operated from a church |
| CHOTA Hunter | For stations that worked multiple CHOTA stations |
| Special Recognition | For outstanding participation over multiple years |

The exact award conditions are announced by WACRAL on the official website each year.

## Getting Started

1. **Choose a church:** Find a church or chapel near you that is suitable for portable radio operations — a garden or churchyard often offers good antenna options.
2. **Obtain permission:** Contact the vicar or church administration and ask for permission to set up an amateur radio station on CHOTA day.
3. **Register:** Check the WACRAL website for information about the current event and register your participation.
4. **Set up your station:** On the second Saturday of September, set up your portable station and get on the air on the usual amateur radio bands.
5. **Submit your log:** After the event, submit your log to WACRAL and apply for a participation certificate.
