# ARLHS — Amateur Radio Lighthouse Society

## What is ARLHS?

The Amateur Radio Lighthouse Society (ARLHS) maintains the World List of Lights (WLOL), the most comprehensive database of lighthouses and lightships for amateur radio purposes. With over 12,000 entries, it serves as the reference database for multiple lighthouse-related programmes including WLOTA and ILLW.

ARLHS promotes amateur radio operations from lighthouses worldwide and provides a standardised reference system used across multiple lighthouse awards.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** 1 contact
- **Activation zone:** At or near the lighthouse
- **Equipment:** Any setup
- **Operating modes:** SSB, CW, FM, all digital modes
- **Special requirements:** The lighthouse must be listed in the ARLHS World List of Lights.

## Points & Awards

Awards are available for both activators and chasers through various associated programmes using ARLHS references.

## Getting Started

1. **Find a lighthouse:** Search the [ARLHS World List of Lights](https://wlol.arlhs.com).
2. **Check access:** Many lighthouses require permission or have restricted access.
3. **Activate:** Set up your station at or near the lighthouse.
4. **Log your contacts:** Use the ARLHS reference number in your logs.
