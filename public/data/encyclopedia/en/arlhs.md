# ARLHS — Amateur Radio Lighthouse Society

## What is ARLHS?

The Amateur Radio Lighthouse Society (ARLHS) is an international organisation that promotes amateur radio operations from lighthouses and lightships. At the heart of ARLHS is the **World List of Lights (WLOL)** — the most comprehensive lighthouse database for amateur radio, with over 12,000 entries worldwide.

ARLHS was founded in the 1990s and has become the central reference database for lighthouse-related amateur radio programmes. WLOL numbers are used as references by many other programmes such as WLOTA and ILLW. Every lighthouse, lightship and light beacon has a unique ARLHS number (e.g. AUT-001).

Lighthouse activations are especially popular among radio amateurs because they often take place at spectacular coastal locations. Many lighthouses are historic structures and tourist attractions, combining radio operations with cultural interest.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** 1 contact
- **Activation Zone:** At or in the immediate vicinity of the lighthouse
- **Equipment:** Any portable or mobile setup
- **Operating Modes:** SSB, CW, FM, all digital modes
- **Special Requirements:** The lighthouse must be listed in the ARLHS World List of Lights (WLOL). Some lighthouses require access permission.

## Points & Awards

| Level | Requirement |
|-------|-------------|
| WLOL Award | 100 different lighthouses worked |
| WLOL 250 | 250 different lighthouses worked |
| WLOL 500 | 500 different lighthouses worked |
| WLOL 1000 | 1,000 different lighthouses worked |

## Getting Started

1. **Find a lighthouse:** Search the [ARLHS World List of Lights](https://wlol.arlhs.com) for lighthouses near you.
2. **Check access:** Many lighthouses are on restricted land — obtain permissions in advance.
3. **Activate:** Set up your station at or near the lighthouse and include the ARLHS reference number in your CQ call.
4. **Log:** Use the ARLHS reference number in your log and report to ARLHS if applicable.

## Austria-specific Notes

Although Austria is a landlocked country, there are occasional historic light beacons on larger lakes. For ARLHS activations, trips to neighbouring countries are a good option — Croatia, Italy and Slovenia have numerous lighthouses along the Adriatic coast.
