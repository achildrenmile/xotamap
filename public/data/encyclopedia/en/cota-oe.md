# COTA-OE — Castles On The Air Austria

## What is COTA-OE?

Castles On The Air Austria (COTA-OE) is the Austrian castle programme, operated by the AFCH (Working Group for Castle Operations in OE) under the OVSV (Austrian Amateur Radio Association). It promotes operating from castles, palaces, fortresses, and ruins in Austria.

COTA-OE maintains its own reference list with hundreds of Austrian castles and palaces. Each reference has a unique number. The programme exists in parallel with the international WCA programme but has its own rules and awards.

Austria possesses an extraordinarily rich cultural heritage of castles — from the Hohensalzburg Fortress to the Riegersburg to the numerous castle ruins in Lower Austria.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** 1 contact
- **Activation zone:** At or near the castle
- **Equipment:** Any portable or mobile setup
- **Operating modes:** SSB, CW, FM, all digital modes
- **Special requirements:** Castle must be in the AFCH/OVSV reference list. Photo documentation recommended.

## Getting Started

1. **Check the reference list:** The COTA-OE reference list is available on the [AFCH website](http://www.afch.at/cota/).
2. **Choose a castle:** Select a castle near you and plan an activation.
3. **Equipment:** A portable setup will do — many castles are accessible by car.
4. **Activate:** Operate from the castle and document with photos.
5. **Submit your log:** Send your ADIF log by email to the COTA-OE manager.

## Austria-specific Notes

COTA-OE is exclusively focused on Austria. The reference list covers castles from all federal states, with particularly rich offerings in Lower Austria, Styria, Carinthia, Salzburg, and Tyrol. The programme offers a wonderful combination of cultural experience and amateur radio.
