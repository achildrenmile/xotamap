# GOTA — Get On The Air

## What is GOTA?

Get On The Air (GOTA) is a special activity category within the ARRL Field Day, the largest annual amateur radio event in North America. GOTA was created to give newcomers, unlicensed individuals and inactive radio amateurs the opportunity to get on the air for the first time — or again — under the supervision of an experienced operator.

ARRL Field Day takes place every year on the fourth weekend in June and is a 24-hour emergency communications and field exercise. GOTA stations are an integral part of this event and allow participants without their own licence to try amateur radio under controlled conditions. This makes GOTA an important recruitment tool for amateur radio.

Unlike most xOTA programmes, GOTA is not a reference-based activation programme. There are no geographic reference numbers or year-round activations. Rather, GOTA is an operating category within Field Day specifically aimed at involving new enthusiasts. Any Field Day group can set up a GOTA station where guests and beginners are mentored.

## Rules

- **Minimum QSOs:** No minimum number; every QSO counts towards the group's Field Day score
- **Activation Zone:** At the respective Field Day station location
- **Equipment:** Provided by the Field Day group; a separate GOTA station is permitted
- **Operating Modes:** All modes allowed at Field Day (SSB, CW, Digital)
- **Special Requirements:** A licensed control operator must supervise the GOTA station at all times; GOTA operators must not hold an active licence of the highest class

## For Hunters/Chasers

Because GOTA is an operating category within ARRL Field Day rather than a reference-based programme, there is no formal hunter/chaser role in the traditional xOTA sense. Any amateur radio operator can work a GOTA station during the Field Day weekend — each QSO counts toward that group's overall Field Day score. There are no separate chaser awards or logs for GOTA contacts. If you work a Field Day station running a GOTA category, simply log the contact as a normal Field Day QSO.

## Points & Awards

| Achievement | Points |
|-------------|--------|
| Each GOTA QSO | Counts as a Field Day point for the group |
| 20+ GOTA QSOs | Bonus points for the overall Field Day score |
| GOTA participation | No standalone GOTA awards; scoring is through Field Day |

## Getting Started

1. Find a local amateur radio club that participates in ARRL Field Day
2. Visit the GOTA station on Field Day weekend (fourth weekend in June)
3. Make your first QSOs under the supervision of a control operator
4. If interested, pursue your own amateur radio licence
